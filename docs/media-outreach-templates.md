## Template 3 — Product Hunt submission

**Name:** Mishwaro — Palestinian Carpooling App

**Tagline:** The first verified carpooling platform for West Bank, Palestine

**Description:**
```
Mishwaro connects drivers and passengers for shared intercity rides across Palestinian cities in the West Bank.

After Careem's exit from Palestine in 2017, millions of Palestinians were left without a reliable, affordable intercity transport option beyond overcrowded shared taxis.

Mishwaro fills this gap with:
🚗 Verified drivers — license, insurance, registration, and two selfies reviewed by admins
💰 Transparent pricing — ₪15–55/seat, set by drivers, no surge pricing
📱 In-app messaging — no need to share phone numbers
🔒 Zero commission — drivers pay a flat monthly subscription
🗺️ Palestinian-first — Arabic UI, ILS currency, checkpoint-aware routing

Available now on iOS App Store. Android coming soon.
```

**First comment:**
```
Hi Product Hunt! 👋

We built Mishwaro because after Careem left Palestine in 2017, the only transport option between cities was informal WhatsApp groups and unverified strangers.

We chose the BlaBlaCar/Poparide model specifically — cost-sharing carpooling, not commercial transport — because it's both more accessible legally and genuinely better for Palestinian commuters.

The hardest part wasn't the tech. It was building trust in a market where people are used to calling a friend-of-a-friend for rides. Our verification system (5-document KYC) was the key to overcoming that.

We'd love your feedback. What features would make you trust this as a daily commuter?
```

## Template 5 — Reddit r/Palestine

**Title:**
```
I built a carpooling app for the West Bank — Mishwaro is now live on iOS
```

**Post:**
```
Hey r/Palestine,

I wanted to share something I've been working on for a while — Mishwaro (مشوارو), a carpooling app specifically for intercity travel in the West Bank.

**The problem:**
After Careem left Palestine in 2017, people went back to informal methods: WhatsApp groups, calling strangers recommended by friends, standing by the road. None of these options give you any safety guarantees or price transparency.

**What Mishwaro does:**
- Connects drivers who are already making a trip (Ramallah→Nablus, Hebron→Bethlehem, etc.) with passengers going the same way
- All drivers go through document verification before publishing any trip
- Price is set by the driver, shown upfront — no negotiating on the roadside
- Cost-sharing model (like BlaBlaCar) — not a taxi service

**Where it works:**
Ramallah, Nablus, Hebron, Bethlehem, Jenin, Tulkarm, Qalqilya, and surrounding areas.

**Download:**
iOS: https://apps.apple.com/dz/app/mishwaro/id6768105898
Web: https://www.mishwaro.com

Would love to hear from anyone who regularly commutes between West Bank cities — what's your biggest frustration with current transport options?
```
